20. František Plánička

Appereances 1042

Major Club: Slavia Prague

František Plánička, “The Cat of Praque”, is one of the most honorable players in history of Czechoslovakian soccer and he's one of the best pre war footballers. His characteristic was sportsmanship as he never once being cautioned or sent off in his career. Although he was of much below-average height for a goalkeeper, he was an effective shot stopper, and his acrobatic style was fulfilled by his superb agility and reflexes.



19. Neville Southall

6. Dino Zoff

Appereances 754

Major Clubs: Napoli, Juventus

Dino Zoff is one of the best goalkeepers the world has ever seen. He debuted his international game in the 1968 UEFA Euro at 26 years of age and is the oldest player to win the World Cup aged 40 when he captained Italy. He is also the goalkeeper to have kept a clean sheet the longest time, from September 1972 to June 1974. He was the pillar of Italy's national team over a decade and became the most iconic player of his nation. Zoff was medium in stature but quick on his feet and with excellent positioning and shot-stopping skills. He was a level-headed goalkeeper, capable of staying calm during the toughest and the most exhilarating moments. Zoff was finished 5th in UEFA Jubilee Poll as the best European footballer in the last 50 years.

2. Gordon Banks

Appereances 752

Major Clubs: Leicester City, Stoke City

Gordon Banks is widely regarded as the greatest goalkeeper in British football history and was the greatest goalkeeper in the world during the second half of the 1960s and early 1970s. His consistent performances in goal led to the re-wording of a common English phrase used in everyday speech "Safe as the Banks of England". His best performance was in the 1970 World Cup where he pulled off an incredible reflex save against a powerful and point blank header of Pele. That save is known as “The save of the century”. Banks is average-sized but quick, agile and possessed with remarkable handling but his strongest asset is his football knowledge and positioning and anticipation. He debut international match at 25 and in total conceded only 57 goals for England national team being the best goalkeeper in the 1966 World Cup where he was a key player on England World Cup winning triumph.


1. Lev Yashin

Appereances 815

Major Club: Dinamo Moscow

Lev Yashin, "The Black Octopus”, is the greatest goalkeeper in history of the game and is regarded as the USSR‘s greatest footballer ever. He is the only goalkeeper to win the Ballon d'Or award (1962). Yashin is known for his athleticism, imposing stature, amazing reflex saves and revolutionizing the goalkeeper role patrolling the area outside the box ("Sweeper Keeper"). During his career, He had stopped 150 penalties which is an all-time record. Moreover, Yashin kept a clean sheets in 65% of his career games. He is a five times Soviet goalkeeper of the year. Yashin was the USSR best player in the 1960 Euros triumph and in the 4th place finish in the 1966 World Cup. In 1994, FIFA established the Lev Yashin Award for the best goalkeeper in World Cup tournament.
