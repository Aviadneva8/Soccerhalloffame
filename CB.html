15. Luis Felipe Monti

Goals 77/Appereances 520

Luis Monti, “The Double Wide”, is considered one of the greatest half backs in the pre-war era. Monti, who possessted great tackling skills and tight and aggresive marking. He played as an attacking centre half in the old-fashioned "Metodo system". He is the only player to represent two different countries in two World Cup finals (losing with Argentina, in 1930 and winning with Italy, in 1934).

7. Gaetano Scirea

Goals 36/Appereances 702

Major Clubs: Atalanta, Juventus

Gaetano Scirea is one of the best defenders the world has ever seen. He was a world-class "Libero" and was the best defender of the world in the 1st half of the 1980s. Scirea was a graceful defender of great vision, ball control and tactical ability. He played the sweeper/"Libero", role for most of his career, and contributed to the development of this position initiated by Franz Beckenbauer in the 1970s. He was a gentleman on the field, never got sent-off or suspended in his career. Scirea is also one of only nine players in the history of the European football that won all three major UEFA competitions.

2. Bobby Moore

Goals 31/Appereances 951

Major Club: West Ham

Bobby Moore is the best defender in English football history and arguably England's best ever player. Moore was admired for his amazing reading of the game, elegent style of play and clean tackling acknowledged by many as the best tackler ever. His tackle on Jairzinho in 1970 World Cup was called "The Tackle of the Century". After the game Pele said "Moore is the best defender I've ever faced". That same year Moore came runner-up in the Ballon d'Or. Moore played mostly as a centre back but due to his great range of passing he led England to 1966 World Cup victory as a sweeper, providing 2 assists in the final.


1. Franz Beckenbauer

Goals 108/Appereances 812

Major Club: Bayern Munich

Franz Beckenbauer, "Der Kaiser", is the best defender of all time and one of the best players the game has ever known. His holds the record for most Ballon d'Or wins for a defender (2) and joint record holder for most appearances in the World Cup "All-Star team" (3). Beckenbauer started as an offensive midfielder in the 1966 World Cup. Between 1966 and 1970 he moved to play as a sweeper and revolutionized football. Using his great tackling and football brain he orchestrated the team. But what stood him out from other sweepers was his intelligence going forward. Having great passing and awareness Beckenbauer was the maestro in W.Germany triumphs in 1972 Euros and 1974 World Cup. Moreover to his ball skills Beckenbauer is noted just as much for his leadership. Those attributes made him one of the most complete footballers of all time.
