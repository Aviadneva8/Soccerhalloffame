Wingers

The wingers are the lattral attackers of the team.
There are RW which play in the right side and LW which play in the left side of defence.

Before reading it's important to understand what is a "Winger".
In the "WM formation" (used in the '20s-'30s), there were two "Inside-Forwards" and two "Outside-Forwards".

Inside-Forwards- are wingers who lined up more centraly, usualy strating on the oposite side of thier strong foot in order to invert towards goal using thier strong foot. Thier job was to score and play through balls to thier teammates.
This role still exsists today.

Outside-Forwards- are wingers who lined up on the touch line (as wide as possible) on the side of their strong foot. Thier job was to wait for the ball to come to them, beat thier marker and deliver the ball to other teammates. Altough, they were rarely scorers good "Outside-Forwards were the best and most attractive players in the team.
This role isn't used tody because of thier lack of work of the ball (the game tody involves more running a trait not common for "Outside-Forwards"). Moreover the Outside-Forwards lack of versatilty resolves managers not using this role.
