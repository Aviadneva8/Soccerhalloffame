Forwards

It has been said that the forwards are the most important position on the pitch.
"Without scoring goals you can't win and forwards provide most the goals."

Forwards are the main goalscoring threat on the team.

In the 19th century forwards were typical "old school" forwards. Big, strog and menace in the air those attributes were key for strikers till the 1950s. By that time managers would understand the importance of a dropping centre forward. A skilled forward whos job was to be involved in the midfield bulid-up play.

The Forwards are divided to 2 roles:
CF (Second Strikers), which apart from their scoring duties they are technical enough to create, assist and roam in the final third.
ST (Strikers), which play much closer to the goal the are the focl point of the attack and they possess natural goalscoring instincts .

It's still fair to say that the common striker is physically more imposing, better in the air but technically inferior that the secont striker.
