8. Phil Neal

Goals 95/Appereances 951

Major Club: Liverpool

Phil Neal is the most decorated player in Liverpool history. Initially signed to play at left-back by Bob Paisley in his first season as manager back in 1974, Neal changed his gaffer's mind eventually and made the right back spot his own. An astute reader of the game and making up for the lack of pace with game intelligence, he was known for his concistency and did not miss a single league game between 14 December 1974 and 24 September 1983, a club-record 365 consecutive games. Neal remains the only Liverpool player to play in all four Champions League wins in the 1970s and 1980s.


7. Javier Zanetti

Goals 30/Appereances 1100

Major Club: Inter

Javier Zanetti, “The Tractor”, is one of the greatest long serving players of all-time. He was an all-round full-back with his strength, resilience, stamina, acceleration, work rate and his ability to run past opposing defenders when joining the attack. He captained Inter for over a decade and holds the most appereances for a non-Italian in Serie A. Although his best years were at right back, he sometimes switched to play as on the left/right midfielder or defensive midfielder. Zanetti is regarded as Inter Milan's greatest player in the 2000s. He was the best defender in Serie A in 1996 according to media rating.


6. Berti Vogts

Berti Vogts, “The Terrier”, is the one of the best European right backs of all-time. He was a key player in the German backline which won every international tournament 1972 Euros and 1974 World Cup. 5. Djalma Santos

Goals 26/Appereances 1099

Major Clubs: Portuguesa, Palmeiras

Djalma Santos was the greatest right back in the Word during the first half 1960s. He was a tough, fast, rugged and uncompromising.
